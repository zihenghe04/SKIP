Sparse Keyframe Interpolation Paradigm
Event-Preserving Sparse-to-Dense Generation for Embodied World Models

The Problem

Dense video world models are expensive

  • Embodied world models predict how actions change the scene — but rollouts are generated frame by frame in pixel space.
  • Long-horizon manipulation videos make this redundant and slow; neighboring frames barely change.
  • You can't just drop frames: policies depend on sparse task-relevant events — approach, contact, grasp, release.
Our Idea

Generate only the keyframes, then interpolate

Stage 1

SKIP-Selector

Event-preserving keyframe selection via multimodal fusion of visual flow, semantic change (DINOv3) and proprioception — with explicit gripper-event protection.

Stage 2

SKIP-Generator

A sparse video diffusion model (Wan 2.2-TI2V-5B, fine-tuned) synthesizes only the selected keyframes from an initial frame + language prompt.

Stage 3

SKIP-Reconstructor

A learned gap predictor sets per-segment frame counts; an action-conditioned interpolator recovers the missing intervals from robot actions.

Takeaways

Sparse keyframes are enough

  • Generate event-preserving keyframes, then reconstruct the dense rollout with action-conditioned interpolation.
  • 4.16× faster than dense generation and −89.0% FVD on LIBERO.
  • SKIP videos match all-real policy training (−1.3 pp sim, −6.7 pp real); dense generation collapses by 48–58 pp.
