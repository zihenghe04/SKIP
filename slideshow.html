01The Bottleneck

Dense pixel rollouts are expensive

  • Generative video world models predict robot rollouts frame by frame, costing roughly 2.5–4.0 s per frame at 50 denoising steps and bf16 precision.
  • Yet most neighboring frames carry near-identical backgrounds and manipulator-motion patterns. Most of that compute is redundant.
  • Dropping frames uniformly does not help. A few pixels of change at gripper closure can decide whether the rollout grasps the object.
Question. Can we keep the policy-level fidelity of dense generation, while skipping the visually redundant intervals?
SKIP
02The Insight

Manipulation videos are sparse events joined by smooth motion

04Architecture

Three modules, two phases

PHASE 1 · TRAIN ONLY
SKIP-Selector
Event-preserving keyframes
Visual, semantic and proprioceptive features fuse into kernel similarity matrices. Kernel temporal segmentation partitions the trajectory, then gripper-event protection locks in contact moments.
PHASE 1 · INFERENCE
SKIP-Generator
Sparse video diffusion
A fine-tuned video diffusion model synthesizes only the selected keyframes from the initial observation and language instruction. No per-frame dense rollout.
PHASE 2 · INFERENCE
SKIP-Reconstructor
Action-guided dense recovery
A learned gap predictor estimates frames between keyframes. An action-conditioned interpolator runs over the corresponding action subsequence to recover a dense, action-aligned rollout.
At inference, only Generator and Reconstructor run. Selector trains the Generator's supervision targets offline.

05What the Selector Sees

Three streams from the same real-robot trajectory

STREAM · VRGB
Raw RGB observation
Third-person RealSense view of the Franka grasping a red mug. Source of motion and appearance.
STREAM · VOPTICAL FLOW
Visual motion magnitude
Pixel-level flow magnitude isolates the moving arm and gripper from the static scene.
STREAM · SDINOv3
Semantic PCA pseudo-color
ViT-L/16 patch tokens projected by PCA. Color changes as the scene crosses semantic boundaries.
These three streams (plus optional proprioception) fuse into a single kernel similarity matrix that KTS segments into event-preserving keyframes. No single stream alone suffices.
A concrete look at what the Selector sees. Same trajectory, three views. Raw RGB carries appearance. Optical flow isolates motion. DINOv3 patch-token PCA captures semantic transitions. All three fuse into one kernel similarity matrix that KTS segments into event-preserving keyframes.

14Takeaways

What SKIP shows

  • A few decisive manipulation events can largely replace dense frame-by-frame generation for embodied world models.
  • Robot-aware multimodal fusion + gripper-event protection identifies these events without supervision and across video-only datasets.
  • Action-conditioned interpolation reconstructs the dense rollout while keeping it physically aligned with the action sequence.
  • Result is a 4.16× cheaper pipeline that, on LIBERO and a four-task Franka platform, sustains near all-real policy performance even when every demo is synthetic.
Open direction. Train vision-language-action models directly on sparse keyframes, testing whether event-aware data preserves policy performance at lower compute.
